Research

My research centers on African digital media and cultural production, focusing on the intersection of social media use among Africans and postcolonial identity. I am particularly interested in how digital platforms influence cultural expressions and identity formations in postcolonial contexts.

Current Projects

  • The Cybernetic Death Machine (ongoing)
    This project investigates the intersection of digital technologies, state power, and queer identity in Nigeria. It examines how state-sponsored violence is mediatized through social media, drawing on Achille Mbembe’s necropolitics and Donna Haraway’s STS-feminist cyborg theory to conceptualize the "Cybernetic Death Machine" (CDM).
  • Vibes and Insha Allah
    This study explores the everyday Nigerian experience as reflected in digital memes. It analyzes how Nigerian netizens perform cultural identities online, demonstrating the cultural performances facilitated by digital media.
